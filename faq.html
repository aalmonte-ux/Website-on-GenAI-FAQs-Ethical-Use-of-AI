What is Generative AI?

Generative AI, sometimes called GenAI, is artificial intelligence (AI) that can create original content such as text, images, video, audio, or software code in response to a user’s prompt or request.

Generative AI relies on sophisticated machine learning models called deep learning algorithms that simulate the learning and decision-making processes of the human brain.

How does it work?

Generative AI works through artificial neural networks designed to mimic some aspects of the human brain, particularly its predictive abilities. These networks consist of layers of “neurons” (or cells) connected by weighted parameters. During training, the AI processes enormous amounts of text token by token, predicting the next token in a sequence. Each prediction is checked against training data, and a backpropagation algorithm adjusts the network’s parameters to improve accuracy. After extensive training, the model can generate coherent and contextually appropriate content. Despite understanding the architecture and training process, researchers do not fully know how generative AI produces its creative outputs, because the internal computations across layers are extremely complex. Some see this intelligence as an emergent property, with models like GPT-4 showing reasoning abilities and broad knowledge akin to human-like understanding, though exactly how this occurs remains a mystery.

What Can GenAI Do? (Strengths)

  • Diverse outputs: Produces seemingly original content and offers multiple perspectives.
  • Leveling the playing field: Simplifies complex ideas, supports multiple languages, and aids those with lower literacy or non-native English speakers.
  • Organizational productivity: Automates tasks like content generation, chatbots, translations, and summaries, saving time and effort.
  • Personalization: Remembers previous interactions, adapts to user style, and enables real-time responses.
  • Industry applications: Integrates into workplace tools (e.g., Microsoft Copilot, Grammarly), enhancing efficiency and learning.

What are its limitations or risks?

  • Lack of trust and authenticity: Can produce inaccurate outputs (“AI hallucinations”) and biased information; requires independent verification.
  • Copyright and ownership issues: Outputs may infringe on existing content; legal and ethical concerns are unresolved.
  • Carbon footprint: Training requires massive energy, contributing significantly to carbon emissions.
  • Feedback loop risk: AI-generated content online can reinforce biases in future AI models, risking “model collapse.”
  • Ethical, social, and human costs: Human reviewers of AI outputs (often low-paid) face exploitation and trauma; AI can replicate biases, limiting diverse viewpoints.
